# VetPath — Business Plan

> **Status:** Prototype / founder-discussion draft. Last updated 2026-07-06.
> **Reminder:** VetPath is education/planning software. It complements — never replaces — VA.gov, VSOs, TAP, and state agencies. All prototype benefit data is SAMPLE data.

---

## 1. Business Model Options (All Three Paths, Fully Built Out)

We are deliberately keeping **all three monetization paths** open until validation tells us which fits the mission and the market. Each is presented fully so a founder can choose or blend.

### Path A — Balanced Mission-First (Free-to-Veteran, B2B2C funded)

**Model:** VetPath is always **free to the individual veteran**. Revenue comes from organizations that already serve veterans and benefit from better-prepared, better-navigated members.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who pays** | VSOs, employers with veteran-hiring programs, base transition offices, universities, workforce boards; supplemented by grants and *ethical* sponsorship. |
| **What they pay for** | Branded/co-branded VetPath instances, cohort dashboards (aggregate, privacy-safe), analytics on transition readiness, priority support, custom resource libraries for their region/base. |
| **Pros** | Aligns with mission; removes cost barrier for veterans; strong trust story; recurring B2B2C contracts. |
| **Cons** | Longer institutional sales cycles; dependent on partner budgets; must keep sponsorship strictly non-exploitative. |
| **Risks** | Sponsor influence over content; perceived commercialization of veterans; grant timing gaps. **Mitigation:** firewall between sponsors and gameplan logic; disclose funding; no benefit "steering." |

### Path B — Lean Commercial / VC-Scalable

**Model:** Classic SaaS with a free tier for veterans plus paid tiers and institutional contracts. Built to scale.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who pays** | Institutions (employers, bases, universities) on SaaS contracts; optionally premium individual/family tiers; privacy-safe, data-informed services (e.g., aggregate transition-readiness benchmarks). |
| **What they pay for** | Seat-based or org-based SaaS tiers, base-transition/employer contracts, advanced planning features, integrations, white-label. |
| **Pros** | Scalable revenue, attractive to VC, clear unit economics, fast iteration. |
| **Cons** | Tension between growth pressure and veteran-first mission; risk of paywalling core value; higher trust bar. |
| **Risks** | Monetizing sensitive data perceived as exploitative; churn if value gated too aggressively. **Mitigation:** never sell personal data; keep core veteran plan free; privacy-safe aggregates only. |

### Path C — Nonprofit / Grant-Funded

**Model:** VetPath operates as (or under) a nonprofit, funded by foundations, government partnerships, and donations. Fully free to everyone.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who pays** | Foundations (veteran-focused philanthropy), VA/state partnership programs, corporate CSR, individual donors. |
| **What they pay for** | Program impact: veterans served, transition outcomes, resource-navigation reach; grant-defined deliverables. |
| **Pros** | Strongest trust and mission alignment; access to veteran-service grant ecosystem; no monetization tension. |
| **Cons** | Grant dependency and reporting burden; slower/limited scaling capital; fundraising is a continuous job. |
| **Risks** | Funding cliffs; mission drift toward funder priorities; limited product velocity. **Mitigation:** diversify funders; build earned-revenue side (Path A) as a hybrid. |

**Recommendation for discussion:** Start **A-leaning hybrid** (free-to-veteran, B2B2C + early grants), keep the SaaS mechanics of B ready to switch on, and keep C viable as a fiscal-sponsorship/nonprofit option if the mission case dominates.

## 2. Customer Segments

- **End users (veterans & families):** transitioning service members (primary), mid-career, disability-navigating, retiring, spouses/dependents.
- **Institutional buyers / sponsors:** VSOs, employers with veteran-hiring goals, base transition offices, universities, workforce boards, state veteran affairs offices, financial-wellness orgs.
- **Funders:** foundations, VA/state partnership programs, corporate CSR, donors.

## 4. Competitive Positioning

VetPath **complements, does not replace** existing resources. We route veterans *to* official help and add the missing "personalized next steps" layer.

| Existing resource | What it does well | Where VetPath adds value (complementary) |
|---|---|---|
| **VA.gov** (incl. former eBenefits functions) | Authoritative benefits info, applications, records | Personalized prioritization + a plan of action across many programs; sends users to VA.gov to verify/apply. |
| **VSO services** (VFW, American Legion, DAV, county CVSOs) | Trusted, accredited claims help and advocacy | Prepares veterans before/after VSO visits; warm handoff for disability/claims. |
| **TAP (Transition Assistance Program)** | Structured separation briefings | Turns TAP content into an individual, trackable 30/60/90 gameplan. |
| **State veteran affairs agencies** | State-specific benefits | Surfaces relevant state benefits (sample in prototype) with "verify" links. |
| **RallyPoint & veteran communities** | Peer networking and support | Not a community app; VetPath focuses on individual planning and can point to communities. |

> We speak about all of these **respectfully and without disparagement.** They are partners in the ecosystem, not targets. VetPath's edge is *personalization + action + verification*, not replacing authoritative sources.

- **VSO chapters** — chapter-level rollout and co-branding.

- **Veteran online communities** — respectful presence, not spam.

## 7. Validation Plan

1. **Problem validation:** Interview 10–15 veterans across segments (start at Frank's meeting). Confirm the "hear it repeatedly, leave without a plan" pain.
2. **Solution validation:** Walk them through the demo; measure whether the generated gameplan feels *personal, clear, and trustworthy*.
3. **Trust validation:** Test disclaimers and "verify at official source" — does it increase or decrease trust? (See `QUESTIONS_FOR_FRANK.md`.)
4. **Distribution validation:** Ask VSOs / transition offices whether they'd share it with members.
5. **Willingness-to-fund validation:** Ask institutions which model (A/B/C) they'd support and why.
6. **Metrics:** intake completion rate, gameplan usefulness rating (1–5), % who say they'd take a next step, % who'd recommend.

## 8. Business Roadmap — 30 / 60 / 90 Day

### 30 Days
- Finalize prototype (Next.js app + standalone HTML demo + sample data).
- Run 8–12 veteran interviews (start with Frank's network).
- Draft one-page pitch for each monetization path (A/B/C).
- Identify 3 candidate VSO/transition-office partners.

### 60 Days
- Iterate gameplan logic and content based on interviews.
- Pilot with one VSO chapter or transition cohort (sample data, clearly labeled).
- Scope the real benefits data pipeline (sources, last-verified dates).
- Get informal legal read on disclaimers / non-advice boundary.

### 90 Days
- Decide primary monetization path (or hybrid) from evidence.
- Sign 1 pilot partnership (LOI or paid pilot).
- Plan v1 with a real (verified, cited) benefits dataset for 1–2 states.
- Establish content-review process (accuracy, boundary, last-verified dates).
