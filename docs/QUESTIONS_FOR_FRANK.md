# Questions for Frank

> **Purpose:** Concrete, conversational questions to validate VetPath with Frank (and, through him, real veterans). Bring the demo. Last updated 2026-07-06.
> **Context to share up front:** VetPath is a demo that turns the benefits info veterans already hear into a personalized 30/60/90 gameplan. It's not the VA, all data is sample, and it always points people to official sources.

---

## (a) The Veteran Audience & Real Pain Points

1. When veterans come to your meetings, what are they actually stuck on — is it *finding* the info, or knowing *what to do next*?
2. Do people leave hearing the same benefits over and over but still without a personal plan? Is that the real gap?
3. Which veterans are hurting most for this — the ones just separating, or mid-career/retiring folks too?
4. What's the #1 thing a veteran wishes someone had handed them on day one after separation?
5. What do people *not* know they're eligible for that they should be?
6. Where do spouses and families fit — are they asking for help too?

## (b) Trust & Credibility

7. When a veteran sees a new app about benefits, what makes them think "this is legit" vs. "this is a scam or a sales pitch"?
8. Does saying clearly "we're not the VA, this is sample data, always verify" build trust — or make it feel unfinished?
9. Would it help or hurt if VetPath is co-branded with a VSO or a name veterans already trust?
10. What's the fastest way to lose a veteran's trust with something like this?

## (c) Design & Readability (Older Veterans)

11. For older veterans, is text size and simplicity a real barrier? What have you seen work?
12. Do calm, professional visuals land better than flags-and-eagles patriotic styling?
13. Should this feel like a form to fill out, or more like a coach walking them through steps?
14. Mobile phone vs. laptop — how do veterans in your circle actually use tech?

## (d) Adoption & Distribution

15. If VetPath were good, how would you get it in front of veterans — at meetings, handouts, a link?
16. What role do meetings like yours play vs. VSO chapters, TAP, and base transition offices?
17. Would a VSO or transition office actually share this with their people? What would they need first?
18. Is word of mouth the real channel here? What makes a veteran recommend something to another veteran?

## (e) Business Model & Who Should Pay

19. Should this always be free to the veteran? (I'm assuming yes.)
20. Who *should* pay — VSOs, employers who want to hire vets, universities, grants, the government?
21. Would it feel wrong to veterans if a sponsor funded it, even if the veteran pays nothing?
22. Nonprofit vs. company — which do you think veterans would trust more here?

## (f) Content Accuracy & Liability

23. How careful do we need to be around disability and claims? My plan is: never give legal advice, always hand off to an accredited VSO.
24. Are there things we should absolutely *not* say or imply?
25. How do veterans react to "verify this at VA.gov" — reassuring, or a cop-out?
26. Who could help us keep the real benefit data accurate and current?

## (g) Validation Questions to Ask Actual Veterans (with the demo in hand)

27. "After this, do you feel like you have a clear plan? What are your next 3 steps?"
28. "Did anything feel personalized to *you*, or generic?"
29. "Was there anything confusing, or any word/acronym you didn't know?"
30. "Would you trust this enough to actually act on it? Why or why not?"
31. "Would you recommend it to another veteran? What would you tell them it does?"
32. "What's missing that would make this genuinely useful for you?"
