01The path

A destination beats a thousand options.

The paralysis isn't laziness — it's four hundred career pamphlets and no ranking. Ten questions produce one honest recommendation with the reasons attached: hands-on or analytical, shop or office, months or years of training. Grounded in O*NET and BLS data, never a black box. For the sample veteran, it reads: Electrician, 93% fit — licensed trade, GI Bill-funded apprenticeship, paid while learning.

02The manifest

Everything you earned, dated and sourced.

Benefits don't fail veterans because they're small. They fail because they're scattered across fifty-one agencies and a dozen federal programs, each with its own window. The manifest puts them in one place — tiered honestly: act now, check eligibility, later.

03The landing

Families transition together, or not at all.

The move, the school calendar, the spouse's license, the distance to a VA medical center — these decide more well-being than any single benefit. The plan treats the household as the unit: checkpoints for who does what, when, and a relocation compare built on official cost, rent, jobs, and VA-access data. And when life changes — new baby, new rating, new city — the plan rewrites itself and shows you the diff before you commit.
