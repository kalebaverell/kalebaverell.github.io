Facilitator guide — running the session

For the person walking a veteran (or a group) through this worksheet. Goal: everyone leaves with 3 goals chosen and at least three concrete next steps written down.

Suggested 30-minute flow

0–5 minWelcome & frame it: “This isn’t the VA telling you what you qualify for — it’s a way to leave today with a written plan you can act on.”
5–12 minPart 1 & 2: fill in the basics and circle up to 3 goals. Encourage picking what feels most urgent, not everything.
12–22 minPart 3: for each goal, write 1–2 things to do in the next 30 days. Keep steps small and specific.
22–28 minParts 4 & 5: note which documents to find and which official source to verify each item with.
28–30 minClose: name the single first step for this week, and who to contact for free accredited help.

Discussion prompts

  1. What’s the one thing you wish someone had handed you when you left service?
  2. Of your three goals, which one, if solved, makes the others easier?
  3. What’s stopped you from acting on this before — time, trust, or not knowing where to start?
  4. Who in your life should have a copy of this plan and your key documents?
  5. Would a tool like VetPath help — and what would make you trust it (or not)?

Ground rules to say out loud

• We don’t confirm eligibility — official sources do. • Accredited VSO help is free; never pay for basic claims assistance. • This is education and planning, not legal, medical, or financial advice.

VetPath is a planning & education tool — not the VA, a law firm, or an accredited claims representative. All benefit information referenced is sample/demo data for this prototype and must be verified through official sources. VetPath does not guarantee any benefit or eligibility.
