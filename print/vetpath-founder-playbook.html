Part 1 · The idea & who it's for

The idea. Veterans hear the same benefits information over and over at meetings and briefings, but walk away without a clear, personalized plan. VetPath turns a veteran's goals, life stage, state, age, and status into a personalized 30/60/90-day gameplan — prioritized benefits, concrete action steps, documents to gather, and links to the official sources to verify each one.

Who it's for (primary → secondary)

  • Primary: service members transitioning out (0–24 months from separation).
  • Secondary: veterans pursuing a job, education, home, or business; those navigating disability/health; those retiring; and spouses/family.

What makes it credible (and what it is not)

It is
A planning, education & navigation tool. It organizes, prioritizes, and points to official/accredited help.
It is not
The VA, a law firm, a claims agent, or an accredited rep. It never confirms eligibility or gives legal/medical/financial advice.
Hard product boundary: veterans, families, benefits, wellness, career, education, housing, entrepreneurship, retirement, and community only. No weapons, tactical/paramilitary/militia content, or political/extremist activity. Crisis resource always present: Veterans Crisis Line — dial 988, press 1.

Part 5 · Money gameplan — how we make money

Keep it free (or low-cost) for veterans; make money from organizations, grants, and sponsors that want to serve veterans. All figures below are illustrative — replace with your own assumptions.

Three revenue paths

Path A — Balanced, mission-first (B2B2C)  Recommended base
Who pays: VSOs, employers with veteran-hiring, universities/veteran-services offices, workforce boards, foundations, ethical sponsors. For: a branded/licensed instance, privacy-safe aggregate analytics, co-branded content, priority support. Veteran pays: nothing.
+ Highest trust, broadest reach.   – Slower revenue; needs partnerships.
Path B — Lean commercial (SaaS)
Who pays: organizations on tiered SaaS (Starter/Pro/Enterprise), base-transition & employer contracts; optional premium convenience features for individuals (document vault, reminders, PDF) — never charging for access to benefits themselves. For: seats, admin dashboards, integrations.
+ Scalable, investor-fundable.   – Must avoid any "pay to access benefits" optics.
Path C — Nonprofit / grant-funded
Who pays: foundation grants, VA/state partnership contracts, corporate veteran-cause sponsorships, individual donations. For: keeping it free and mission-locked.
+ Maximum trust; grant access.   – Grant dependency; slower product velocity.
Recommended: a hybrid. Run mission-first (free to veterans) funded by organization licenses (Path A) + grants/sponsorship (Path C), and add optional premium convenience features (Path B) later once trust is established. Decide together in Part 4.
Recommended pricing — organization licenses (veterans always free): Small (local VSO / nonprofit) $2,500/yr · Mid (university / workforce board) $7,500/yr · Enterprise (employer / state agency) $20,000/yr. Give your first 1–2 partners a free or discounted pilot in exchange for feedback and a testimonial.
